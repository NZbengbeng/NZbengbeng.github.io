audio-visualiser

This is something I am still planning. Initially I wanted to create an audio visualiser on the touchbar to sync with Spotify (using Swift and other tools). After looking around it seemed a bit difficult as the TouchBar is NOT recommended to be used as an output device. Now I am currently exploring 3 options:

  • A real-time audio visualiser which reads in the audio output (most likely will have a few ms of delay).

  • An offline audio visualiser which reads in the audio file beforehand.

  • Use Spotify Web API to make a real-time audio visualiser.

    • As it is my first time working in this environment I might just make an offline audio visualiser in Swift or Python first.

    part-4-project

    As part of the undergraduate engineering degree all students are required to conduct a research project in their final year. The title of our project was "Probabilistic Sensor Fusion For Localisation and Mapping in GPS-denied areas." But really we didn't have time to get up to the probabilistic sensor fusion aspect. This project allowed my partner and I to work with lidars and inertial measurement units, real firefighters, gather experimental data, and use ROS to produce an initial prototype. Feel free to check out a few project deliverables: literature review, final report, compendium, poster, and video (note: this isn't my official YouTube channel).
